1 Day Culture Experience at Gorilla Guardians Village

Tour Overview

The Gorilla Guardians Village is located adjacent to Volcanoes National Park in Kinigi, Musanze district, Northern Province. A visit to the Gorilla Guardians Village to share with local people home and heritage treasures including lifestyles, activities, artifacts, and ways of living of local Rwandan people. This village gives a rare chance to meet local people in their environment with a taste of Rwandan culture and traditions.

Culture experience at Gorilla Guardians Village and a visit to the Ellen DeGeneres Campus of the Dian Fossey Gorilla Fund-Transfer back to Kigali.

Our driver-guide will pick you up from your hotel/residence in Kigali, short briefing about your tour and drive to Kinigi in Musanze near Volcanoes National Park to Gorilla Guardians Village, the drive form Kigali city to Musanze last approximately 2h30. You will enjoy the lovely beauty of the unwinding rolling landscape of Rwanda countryside that makes it famous as a country of a thousand hills.

In a short time, you will be the hearts of Gorilla Guardians Village, a beautiful project empowering the community and providing alternatives to poaching. You will be joined by local guide who will welcome you to the village and takes you through an insight into the cultural history of Rwanda. The guide will take you through some of the programs such as visiting the huts, local beer production, marriage ceremony, storytelling, and rap it with fantastic dancers and drummers, who will showcase their traditional dance before you, and you can join.

After this amazing experience transfer to the Ellen DeGeneres Campus of the Dian Fossey Gorilla Fund to learn more about mountain gorilla conservation.

Adjacent to the Volcanoes National Park, the campus has three main buildings: the Sandy and Harold Price Research Center, the Cindy Broder Conservation Gallery, and the Rob and Melani Walton Education Center, as well as housing for visiting students and researchers. An extensive living laboratory was created on the former agricultural site through the planting of more than 250,000 native plants and the use of green roofs, rainwater harvesting, and a constructed wetland for wastewater treatment.

From the outset, the mission of this campus was focused on creating a space to engage the many stakeholders in conservation, students, scientists, tourists, conservation partners, community members to advance our collective goal of saving gorillas and more broadly, the planet'. The beauty of the campus elevates people's thinking about conservation, helping them realize how important conservation is.

After this amazing experience, you will have lunch here and later, transfer back to Kigali city straight to your hotel/residence or to Kigali International Airport for your departure flight.
