2 Days Chimpanzee and Canopy Walk Tour

Tour Overview

This tour will give you a great chance to do a canopy walk and chimpanzee trekking in Nyungwe National Park. Nyungwe is a home to habituated chimpanzees and 12 other primates species (including 400-strong troop habituated Ruwenzori Black & White Colobus), it's also a birdwatcher's with over 300 species.

Day 1: Transfer to Nyungwe National Park-Canopy Walkway Experience

Early morning at 5h00 am, our driver-guide will pick you up from your hotel in Kigali, short briefing about your tour and drive to Nyungwe National Park in the south western part of Rwanda where it is borders by the Rwanda-Burundi border in the south and the Democratic Republic of Congo lies on the west side of the park. It sites on an area of about 1020 sq.km and has an elevation of 1600-2950m above the sea level. The drive from Kigali to Nyungwe National Park is approximately 5 hours.
Along the way to Nyungwe National Park stop off in the Cultural Heritage Corridor of Rwanda and visit the King's Palace Museum. After this amazing experience continue to Nyungwe National Park, arrive at Uwinka Park office for the adventure of Canopy walk which is very exciting experience but not for the faint hearted.

The canopy walk consist of three bridges and three towers, the bridge leading to the first tower is 37 meters long and 20 meters high, the main bridge has a length of 86 meters and a height of 60 meters, the final exit bridge is 25 meters long and a height of 15 meters. The total length of the canopy walk is 150 meters. Nyungwe forest has about 280 bird species, 25 of which are endemic, Nyungwe is one of the most important and still undiscovered bird watching destinations of Africa. This amazing tour of canopy walk in Nyungwe National Park last 2 hours with full relaxation.

After your canopy walk experience head back to Uwinka Park reception, transfer to your accommodation lodge/hotel, check-in, have your dinner and overnight.
Accommodation option: Nyungwe Top View Hill Hotel (Luxury), Emeraude Kivu Resort (Mid-range), Chimpanzee Lodge (Standard), and Nyungwe Nziza Ecolodge (Budget).

Day 2: Chimpanzee trekking-Transfer back to Kigali

Wake up early to go chimpanzee tracking in Nyungwe National Park. After a short briefing by your guide, walk through stunning tea plantations before entering the thick luscious forest. There are 13 species of primate found in the forest and excellent birding opportunity with over 100 species of botflies and the occasional chameleon.
The excursion can last from one to four hours but you will definitely hear the chimpanzees from the moment you enter the forest. Once found you will stay an hour with the chimpanzees, watching them play, fight and eat in the atmospheric forest. Return to the base and transferred to the reception.

After lunch, meet your driver-guide ready to get back to Kigali in the hotel/residence or at Kigali International Airport if the flight is scheduled in the evening.
