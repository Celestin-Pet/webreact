1 Day Kigali City Tour

Tour overview

Ideally positioned in the centre of Rwanda, Kigali extends across several hills and valleys, with good road links to the rest of the country. The verdant capital city is pleasantly low key yet dynamic and progressive, with just over one million inhabitants. First-time visitors to the bustling and colourful centre often remark how clean the streets are, a matter of pride across every community throughout the country.
Kigali is a relatively new city. It was founded as an administrative outpost in 1907, and became the capital of the country at independence in 1962, shifting focus away from Huye.

Day trip: Kigali city tour

Kigali city tour will offer you with one of the best views of Kigali, past and present, assuring that one feels they genuine heartbeat of the city. You will get to visit some of its precious historical sites which leaves you wanting more. You might have not taken time to explore deep into the beauty of this city but this short tour will give you a feel of the highlights of Kigali; a city that is blessed with a moderate climate throughout the year.
Kigali is not only a vibrant commercial center but also a rich cultural center full of amazing historical sites. Its beauty is well witnessed in the evening and at night all lights glimmer over the magnificent hills. The life in Kigali is exceptional with numerous things to keep you entertained and relaxed in beautiful hotels.

You will visit also the famous Kimironko market, the busiest market in town. The entrance to the market is small but when you walk through the gates, it's a shopping paradise. The market is in a large warehouse complex, with four equidistant entrances. Locals from all over the city come here to soak up on fruits, vegetables, fabrics clothing, shoes and general household's necessities.
No visit to Kigali would be complete without a visit to the Kigali Genocide Memorial, which, through education and peace-building, honours the memory of the more than one millions Rwandans killed in 1994. The three permanent exhibitions and burial gardens form part of a meaningful tribute to those who perished, and provide a powerful educational tool for visitors.
